### Scene 1 — Arrival: The Road Into Harrow Fen

The players are traveling through farmland at dusk — passing through, not headed here specifically. The village of **Harrow Fen** sits at the end of a dirt track bordered by wheat fields. By the time it comes into view, something is wrong.

> The smell reaches you first — smoke, and something underneath it, something cold that doesn't belong in a summer evening. Then you hear it: shouting, a dog cut short mid-bark, and a sound like wind through a hollow place, except the air is still.
>
> The village is burning in patches. Not all of it — not yet. You can see figures running between buildings. Some of them are villagers. Some of them move wrong.

The attack is already in progress. Players enter Harrow Fen from the south road. The village gate is open and unguarded — the two gate guards are visible at the edge of the torchlight, one unconscious on the ground, one backing away from something in the shadows.

#### The Creatures

The creatures are **Drawn Shades** — entities native to the Nullshore Expanse that have been pulled through the forestall's inversion. They are not attacking with intent. They are disoriented, drawn toward the strongest magical signal in the village, and reacting with aggression to anything that gets between them and it.

They appear as tattered silhouettes — shapes that suggest a figure without committing to one. They move wrong: too fluid, too continuous, no joint articulation. They do not speak. They pass through closed doors without opening them.

> **D&D Beyond note:** Drawn Shades are homebrew creatures and must be added as a homebrew entry. See the Drawn Shades stat block in lore.md.

#### Combat — The Gate

If players engage, two creatures are harassing the gate guard. Village guard **Pol** (see NPC roster) is trying to drag his unconscious partner to safety. This is the players' introduction to combat — small scale, manageable, with an immediate rescue objective.

After the gate skirmish, the village guard captain arrives with four militia members and pushes the remaining creatures back. The attack begins to ebb as the creatures are repelled toward the gateway.

### Scene 2 — The Forestall Ritual

Players are brought to the center of Harrow Fen where the **forestall stone** stands — a weathered granite pillar about eight feet tall, carved with interlocking sigil-work worn smooth by generations of hands. The lower third is grey granite. The upper third is the same. But the middle third transitions into a column of clear crystal, and it is not still.

Slow ribbons of dark blue ink coil through the crystal in languid, looping arcs — unhurried, almost beautiful, the kind of motion that belongs in deep water. At the center of the crystal, running its full vertical length, is a long black filament: jagged, irregular, like a crack left by something that struck the stone from the inside. The blue ribbons are not coiling freely. They are being drawn toward it — feeding into the filament in a slow, continuous pour, the way water finds a crack in a dam.

At the base of the crystal, thin metal wires are embedded into the stone, running down the length of the granite below and terminating at a small metal box fixed to the pillar's base. The box is utilitarian — bolted housing, no ornamentation — but on its front face is a single small crystal that flickers faintly and irregularly, like a dying lamp struggling to hold its charge.

Six villagers in plain robes kneel around it, palms pressed flat to the stone, murmuring in unison. A seventh stands at the head: **Serath** (see NPC roster), the ritual keeper. Her eyes are closed. The amber light brightens with each pass of the chant.

> The air near the stone is warm. You can feel the pulse of it in your chest, a low hum that sits just below hearing. Whatever this thing is, it is working. The creatures that were pressing into the village are gone — pulled back, or gone to ground.
>
> Serath opens her eyes. She looks exhausted. "Third time today," she says to no one in particular. "The barrier holds. We just have to keep holding it."

If players ask about the ritual, Serath or any villager can explain: the forestall used to require a communal renewal **once a month**. In the last six weeks, the demand has escalated to **three times a day**. The participants rotate where they can, but the pool of willing and capable villagers is small and the exhaustion is visible on all of them. Several have collapsed during rituals and had to be carried off. No one has stopped.

Players who succeed on a DC 12 Arcana check notice something subtle: the pulse of the stone isn't pushing outward. It's pulling — inward, toward the north end of the village, toward the direction the creatures came from. This detail is available, but not explained.

### Scene 3 — The Hiring

**Elder Maren** (see NPC roster) meets the players in the village hall — a long, low building that currently serves as a triage point for the injured. She is direct and doesn't waste time.

> "You're not from here and that's good. People from here won't go. I need someone who doesn't know what they'd be losing."
>
> She spreads a rough map on the table. A circle marks the village. A dotted line leads north to an X labeled *the breach*.
>
> "Six weeks ago they started coming. Every night — up to three of them, sometimes fewer. They don't break in. They don't climb walls. They just... come through doors. Any door. It doesn't matter if it's locked. We don't know where they're coming from or how to stop it at the source. The forestall is the only thing keeping them from overrunning us, but it's not enough. Whatever is sending them needs to be stopped from the other side."

She offers: 200 gold per person, a guide who knows the other side, and whatever supplies they need from the village stores.

#### What the Players Are Told
- Creatures have been appearing nightly for six weeks — up to three per night
- They enter through doors — any door, regardless of locks or barriers
- The forestall requires renewal three times a day now — it used to be once a month. The village is exhausted.
- The forestall is weakening under the strain (this is false — Maren believes it, but it isn't what's happening; the increased demand is the false house drawing more energy, not the forestall degrading)
- The breach must be sealed from inside — the gateway can't be closed from this side
- A guide in the supply town of **Caldport** (one day's travel) knows the Nullshore and can take them through

#### What Is Withheld (GM knowledge only)
- The forestall is not weakening — it is fully functional, but its signal has been hijacked
- A false house called **The Gilded Hall** absorbed a living arcanist and held them inside, still connected to their portal equipment. Through that equipment, The Gilded Hall latched onto the forestall's signal across the byway boundary and inverted it — the forestall's repulsive force now pulls energy toward the Nullshore instead of pushing threats away
- Every time the ritual is performed, The Gilded Hall feeds. The more the village fights back, the stronger it becomes
- The creatures aren't attacking — they are being pulled toward the inverted forestall signal and react with aggression when they arrive
- Serath suspects something is wrong with the forestall's behavior but has not said so to Maren, because she has no explanation

### NPC Roster — Harrow Fen

#### Elder Maren
- **Role:** Village leader, quest giver
- **Personality:** Controlled, practical, frightened underneath. Has made hard decisions before. Doesn't show weakness in front of strangers.
- **What she knows:** Everything the village knows, which isn't much. She believes the forestall is failing.
- **What she wants:** The breach sealed. The attacks to stop. Her village to survive.
- **D&D Beyond:** Use as a standard NPC (no stat block needed for Act 1).

#### Serath
- **Role:** Ritual keeper, forestall guardian
- **Personality:** Devout, precise, protective of the forestall as an institution. Has doubts she won't voice.
- **What she knows:** The forestall's behavior has changed slightly over the past three weeks — the draw direction has shifted. She has logged it but told no one.
- **What she wants:** To believe the ritual is working. To not be the person who tells the village it might be making things worse.
- **Secret:** Her log of the anomaly is in her cottage. Players who search it can find it (DC 14 Investigation). It contains two weeks of entries noting the inward pull.
- **D&D Beyond:** Use as a standard NPC.

#### Pol
- **Role:** Gate guard, first friendly face
- **Personality:** Young, shaken, grateful. Talks too much when nervous.
- **What he knows:** He's seen the creatures every night for a week. They always go toward the center of the village — toward the stone. He's noticed that too, but hasn't connected it to anything.
- **D&D Beyond:** Use as a Guard stat block (MM) if combat stats are needed.

#### Captain Duvesh
- **Role:** Militia captain
- **Personality:** Weathered, blunt, professionally calm. Respects competence immediately and ignores everything else.
- **What he knows:** Tactical picture only. Creature patterns, attack timing, casualty count (4 dead, 11 injured over six weeks).
- **What he wants:** Reinforcements or a solution. Preferably both.
- **D&D Beyond:** Use as a Veteran stat block (MM) if combat stats are needed.
