# Plot Structure - Spoiler-Free Collaborator Version

This file is safe to share with a potential player who is helping with campaign design. It preserves the public premise, pacing, tone, and open design work while omitting GM-only causes, secrets, late-campaign revelations, final encounter mechanics, and ending consequences.

## Collaboration Boundaries

Player collaborators can safely help with:

- Village atmosphere, non-secret NPC presentation, and social scenes
- Travel scenes, supply town texture, equipment lists, and local rumors
- Nullshore sensory descriptions that do not explain the mystery
- Encounter pacing, terrain descriptions, and D&D Beyond-compatible encounter setup
- Player handouts that contain only information characters could reasonably learn
- Maps, visual references, props, and table aids

Avoid discussing or designing:

- The true cause of the Harrow Fen crisis
- The real nature or objective of the final threat
- The exact function of the forestall beyond what villagers believe
- Act 5 revelations
- Final encounter phases, win conditions, or epilogue outcomes

## Pacing Target

Target total play time: 4 hours.

Suggested spoiler-free pacing:

- Act 1 - Harrow Fen: 35-45 minutes
- Act 2 - Caldport: 30-40 minutes
- Act 3 - Nullshore Exploration: 75-95 minutes
- Act 4 - Approach: 25-35 minutes
- Act 5 - Late-campaign discovery and finale: 60-80 minutes

If the session runs long, compress Caldport preparation, reduce optional exploration beats in Act 3, and move quickly from the approach into the finale.

## Act 1 - The Village (0-15%)

The players arrive at Harrow Fen during an attack. The village is frightened, exhausted, and relying on a communal protective ritual to survive.

### Player-Facing Premise

- Harrow Fen is under repeated attack by strange creatures.
- The village's protective ward, called the forestall, is under heavy strain.
- The villagers believe the source of the attacks lies beyond the village.
- The players are hired to investigate and stop the threat.

### Key Scenes

#### Scene 1 - Arrival: The Road Into Harrow Fen

The party enters Harrow Fen at dusk as smoke rises from parts of the village. Villagers are fleeing, guards are overwhelmed, and strange shadow-like creatures are moving through doors and buildings in unnatural ways.

Design goals:

- Establish urgency immediately.
- Let the players rescue a guard or civilian.
- Introduce the creatures without fully explaining them.
- Keep the first combat small and manageable.

#### Scene 2 - The Forestall Ritual

The players witness villagers reinforcing the forestall stone through a communal ritual. The ritual works, but it is clearly taking a toll. Villagers are exhausted, frightened, and trying not to admit how close they are to collapse.

Design goals:

- Show the village's dependence on the ritual.
- Make the forestall feel old, sacred, and partly technological.
- Present a mystery: the system is functioning, but something about the situation is wrong.
- Avoid explaining the underlying cause.

#### Scene 3 - The Hiring

Elder Maren asks the party to travel beyond Harrow Fen and investigate the source of the attacks. She offers payment, supplies, and a contact who can guide them further.

What the players can be told:

- Creatures have appeared repeatedly for six weeks.
- They can pass through closed doors and normal barriers.
- The forestall now requires renewal multiple times a day.
- The village believes the protective system is weakening.
- A guide in Caldport can help the party reach the next stage of the investigation.

### Public NPC Roster - Harrow Fen

#### Elder Maren

- Role: Village leader, quest giver
- Personality: Controlled, practical, frightened underneath
- Public knowledge: The village is under threat and needs outside help
- D&D Beyond: Standard NPC, no stat block needed unless improvised combat occurs

#### Serath

- Role: Ritual keeper, forestall guardian
- Personality: Devout, precise, protective of the forestall
- Public knowledge: The ritual has become more demanding and the village cannot sustain it forever
- D&D Beyond: Standard NPC

#### Pol

- Role: Gate guard, first friendly face
- Personality: Young, shaken, grateful, talkative when nervous
- Public knowledge: The attacks are terrifying and the creatures behave unnaturally around doors
- D&D Beyond: Use the official Guard stat block if combat stats are needed

#### Captain Duvesh

- Role: Militia captain
- Personality: Weathered, blunt, professionally calm
- Public knowledge: Tactical information about attack timing, casualties, and village defenses
- D&D Beyond: Use the official Veteran stat block if combat stats are needed

## Act 2 - Supply Town (15-30%)

The players travel to Caldport to prepare, gather supplies, and meet people who know how to navigate the dangerous space beyond ordinary roads.

### Player-Facing Goals

- Let players choose gear and ask questions.
- Introduce guides, fishermen, or other locals with practical experience.
- Explain enough survival rules to make the next act playable.
- Seed strange sensory details without revealing the full mystery.

### Safe Design Areas

- Caldport's look, economy, docks, inns, markets, and local superstition
- A guide NPC with strong personality and practical knowledge
- Rumors about travelers who vanish, return changed, or refuse to speak
- Equipment that is useful but D&D Beyond-compatible
- A short travel scene from Harrow Fen to Caldport

---

## Act 3 - Nullshore Exploration (30-65%)

The players enter the Nullshore Expanse, a dangerous interstitial sandbar between two impossible seas. Exploration teaches them how this place behaves before the central threat becomes clear.

### Player-Facing Goals

- Make the Nullshore feel beautiful, hostile, and wrong.
- Teach players that sound, shelter, water, and distance cannot be trusted normally.
- Provide at least one safe or semi-safe teaching encounter.
- Include one meaningful combat or hazard encounter.
- Give players clues that point them toward the next location.

### Known Safe Setting Facts

- The Nullshore is an endless sandbar between a red sea and a blue sea.
- The seas are dangerous and should not be entered.
- Sound carries strangely.
- Some structures are not what they appear to be.
- Storms can change the landscape and increase danger.

### Safe Encounter Concepts

- Arrival at a ruined entry point
- Encounter with a guide, fisherman, or stranded traveler
- Investigation of an apparently safe structure
- Sandbar creature encounter using official D&D Beyond stat blocks where possible
- First storm event as narrative pressure rather than a custom rules system

---

## Act 4 - Approach (65-80%)

The players locate the place tied to the crisis. The environment becomes more contradictory, the storm pressure rises, and the expedition shifts from exploration into confrontation.

### Player-Facing Goals

- Build dread through environmental contradictions.
- Signal that the party is nearing something powerful.
- Give players enough clues to feel they are choosing to continue.
- Keep any hazards D&D Beyond-compatible.

Safe design areas:

- Approach terrain
- Weather escalation descriptions
- Sounds and visual details that feel wrong
- Optional minor hazards or creature pressure

---

## Act 5 - Late-Campaign Discovery And Finale (80-100%)

This section contains major revelations and should remain GM-only in the full outline.

### Spoiler-Free Player-Collaborator Summary

The party discovers the deeper nature of the crisis, learns that the problem is larger than Harrow Fen first understood, and must make a final high-pressure choice during the confrontation.

Safe design areas:

- General tone of discovery: awe, dread, and urgency
- Non-specific visual motifs for unstable rooms, old artifacts, and impossible doors
- Emotional reactions of NPCs who realize the situation is worse than expected
- Escape pacing and post-finale atmosphere, without defining outcomes

Do not share:

- The exact revelation
- The final threat's mechanics
- The available final choices
- Which outcome is best, worst, or incomplete
- Any explanation of what is really happening to the forestall

## Themes

These themes are safe to discuss with a player collaborator:

- Deception vs truth
- Protection under strain
- Familiar places becoming unsafe
- Exploration of impossible geography
- The danger of trusting first impressions
- Ordinary people trying to survive something they do not understand

## D&D Beyond Compatibility Notes

- Use official 5e monster, item, spell, and condition names whenever possible.
- Flag anything that requires homebrew entry before it is used in prep.
- Avoid encounter mechanics that require custom action systems, custom conditions, or unusual boss tracking unless the GM approves them.
- Storms and environmental pressure should usually be narrative pacing tools, not new mechanical subsystems.
